## Support should never feel like spying.

## Trust is the product

Families will only bring Jude into the home if it feels respectful.

The website should clearly explain permissions, approved contacts, alert settings, and what information is shared.

Privacy is not a footnote; it is a selling point.

**Image direction:** Warm home setting with Jude on the wall, natural light, family details, and a calm senior-friendly interface.

## Meaningful alerts, not constant surveillance

Jude should focus on helpful events like missed medication, no morning check-in, emergency requests, or unusual patterns.

Families do not need a minute-by-minute feed of someone’s life.

Seniors deserve dignity and control.

**Image direction:** Warm home setting with Jude on the wall, natural light, family details, and a calm senior-friendly interface.

## Clear family permissions

The senior and family should understand who receives updates and why.

Different contacts can receive different types of notifications.

Trust grows when the system is easy to explain.

## FAQs

### Will Jude share everything with family?
No. The website should position Jude around configurable sharing and meaningful alerts.

### How should privacy be described?
Use simple, direct language: the family decides what Jude tracks, who gets notified, and when alerts happen.

### Is Jude meant to replace family or professional care?
No. Jude is designed to support seniors, families, and caregivers with reminders, communication, coordination, and home routines.
